How do we count our anonymity rating?

You PC anonymity rating allows to quickly show all the vulnerabilities and flaws in your defenses. We divide these flaws into three approximate groups: serious, moderate, and minor.

 

The serious flaws group consists of unmistakable efforts on your part to hide or change your actual IP address or its leak. You may reveal yourself due to bad anonymity techniques or their inaccurate usage. It does not always mean that someone will surely know your IP, but it means that there is at least a reason to track you and try to know it. This group consists of Tor, anonymizers, proxies, WebRTC leaks, and DNS differences.

 

The moderate flaws group involves indirect signals that do not reveal univocal efforts on your part to hide yourself, but they are alarming. These are various browser heading, local time difference between your city and your system, language difference between your region and your system. It allows to know with a high degree of probability that you are trying to hide your actual location.

 

The minor flaws group consists of issues that are worth your attention and fixing: blacklisting your IP address, using plugins that are essentially separate applications which may trigger data leakage (Flash, Java, ActiveX), and deactivated DoNotTrack browser option which prevents websites from tracking you.

 

Upon detecting any of these anonymity leaks, your system will penalize you with:

 

30 points for using Tor, anonymizers, proxies, and DNS server differences

20 points for differences in the system time, language, and browser headings

10 points for using plugins Flash, Java, ActiveX, blacklisting your IP, detected proxy ports

5 points for disabled DoNotTrack

 

The formula with a 0.75-0.1 coefficient highlights the importance of a problem or its complex nature in case of several leaks.
